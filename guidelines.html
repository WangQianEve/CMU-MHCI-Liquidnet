Guidelines
We conceptualized findings from secondary research and analogous domains into design guidelines that summarize the state-of-the-art practices.
Getting Attention
1. Set a high threshold for firing an alarm in an intense environment

Attention is the most valuable resource traders have, and the value of an alert system is to help traders allocate their attention efficiently in a not annoying fashion. As every alert is an interruption to the on-going tasks, only be alarming when the alert requires immediate attention and present the rest of the alerts calmly. In the long run, the system will gain credibility for its accuracy and trust from traders.

2. Increase situational awareness with an ambient alert

The ambient alert is a peripheral interface used continuously in the background. It does not prompt an immediate reaction but will increase situational awareness. For example, life monitoring in hospitals updates the screen calmly but continuously. It doesn't interfere with what nurses are doing, but can provide closure for patients' situations whenever they look at the screen.

Air Traffic Control Dashbaord
3. Design for repetitive tolerance

A sound and animation may be pleasant when the first time, but consider what may users feel if they need to interact with it 100 times a day. To design for repetitive tolerance, the sound and the associated animation should not last longer than 0.3 seconds.

Getting Attention
1. Using visual hierarchy to guide users to process information

Information hierarchy gives users an intuitive guide of how they should navigate the information presented to them. By chunking information into bite-size, users can consume the alerts more efficiently, especially in a busy environment where users have short attention spans for incoming information.

Example for visual hierarchy. Source: ScienceDirect
2. Having a clear call-to-action

Call-to-action (CTA) is a marketing term referring to interactions designed to prompt immediate actions. Call-to-action is usually a button that is saturated in color, more prominent than other elements, and there should only be one CTA at a time.

The call to action button on Spotify’s mobile homescreen
3. Communicate smart by giving a summary and highlights

Apply effective communication between people to the alert system. A person's working memory can hold seven items. When there are many alerts of the same type, provide a summary to reduce the effort for processing the information for users. Present a high-level overview. Then Details can be provided after users expand the notifications.

Grouped notifications from Gmail’s mobile application

Getting Attention
1. Providing alarm data and report

Whether alarm fatigue is happening can be measured with the signal detection theory. If users are missing too many signals, either alarm fatigue is becoming an issue or the system is not presenting the alarm effectively. In a clinical alarm management system, weekly reports are provided to let managers identify trends and adjust their practices. Content in the report may include total weekly alarms per unit, duration of alarms by types, duration to respond to nurse calls, etc.

Signal Detection Matrix
2. Suspend alerts that do not require immediate action

A powerful way to reduce alarm fatigue is to delay forwarding information that does not require immediate action, or only send over a summery during users' downtime. At Johns Hopkins Health System, crisis alarms are sent immediately to the nurse, while technical alarms (e.g., lead failures or bed disconnects) can be delayed or sometimes auto-corrected or silenced. During one week, 61,227 warning levels alarms on our physiological monitors, only 3,103 of these were messaged out to mobile devices.

3. Customize alarm threshold and calibrate continuously

Setting a threshold for firing an alert and directing alerts only to accountable personnel are powerful ways to reduce alarm fatigue. At Johns Hopkins Health System, the alert algorithm is customized based on the demographics of the patients, clinical workflows, time of the day, staffing schedules ... etc. Nurses are encouraged to adjust the alert threshold without physicians' orders to make monitor more actionable.

Customization
1. Having a good default

Customization lets users make their selections about what they want to see, or set preferences for organizing and displaying information. First of all, make the system usable and useful without customization. Many users to do not use the customization even if the feature we. Customization should be an add-on to an already enjoyable experience rather than asking users to fix the experience themselves.

Many users to do change the default wallpaper on their desktop or mobile phone
2. Make it easy to discover

Since configuring the system is not users’ primary goal, users will not look for customization and use it for the sake of using it. Customization features should be placed near the content they relate to and be presented in a manner in which users can see how it will assist with their primary goal in the system.

LinkedIn allows users to customize what notification users want to receive. “Turn off” is placed close to the items and is easy to discover.
3. Layering the customization features

When there is a rich option set, avoid overwhelming users. Only show users the most useful customization options at first and deferred other options to “advanced options.”

Slack provides a set of color themes for users to select from, hiding the advanced options behind a button.

Building Trust
1. Build Trust Gradually

Many systems need users’ data to function, and getting data from users requires trust and commitment. Let users experience the product first, and do not be too eager to get data from users upfront. If they feel negative upon first trying it out, they might never try again. After users experience the product and benefit from it, encourage users to share data gradually. For example, in the trading scenario, traders will feel more comfortable about automating course correction after taking Liquidnet’s advice and resulting in successful trades.

The honestbee website ask for user’s personal information before letting users to experience the product
2. Explain the key component of how the system works

When a system suggests actions to traders, they need a certain level of understanding of how the system works in order to take the suggestion. The explanation can include but not limited to data sources, mechanism of algorithms, confidence level, potential outcome, restrictions. Traders need the right amount of information, and a balance needs to be found between providing too little information and being verbose.

Amazon explains why it recommend certain items concisely.
